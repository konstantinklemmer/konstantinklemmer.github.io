Research

Exploring the spatio-temporal dynamics of carsharing

Where and when do people use carsharing? We address this question by looking at where people drive and how points of interest (POIs) in the surrounding area might help us understand what the purposes of trips are. This project was published as a conference paper at AMCIS 2016 in San Diego and can be found here.

Predictive analytics for carsharing

This project aims at predicting carsharing demand patterns and providing valuable decision support to carsharing operators. Using machine learning techniques and statistical analysis on an Amsterdam dataset, we train a prediction algorithm for urban carsharing activity. We evaluate the quality of this approach with test data from a significantly different urban area: Berlin. Comparing predicted and actual carsharing activity, we observe strong predictive power of the forecasting model. A paper based on this research has been published in Decision Support Systems.

Introduction to Machine Learning in R

This tutorial is the transcript of a Machine Learning workshop I hosted as a part of the Imperial College Data Science Society (ICDSS) talent development programme. This document targets Machine Learning beginners and requires some elementary experience with R.
